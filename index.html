About NASHCON

Shaping Future Leaders Through Diplomacy...

NashCon Model United Nations is an annual, non-institutional conference that engages participants in simulated United Nations committees. Our mission is to educate participants about international relations, current global issues, and the art of diplomacy.

Founded in 2025, NashCon is known for its innovative committee designs, expert guest speakers, and commitment to fostering meaningful debate.

This year's conference will feature a special Crisis Genesis Council and opportunities to connect with students from diverse backgrounds who share a passion for global affairs.

Our Committees

Crisis Genesis Council

A crisis committee is a decision-making body that has more power than a traditional committee. Whilst General Assemblies recommend and build consensus, focusing on creating and refining frameworks for the nations involved to align their actions, crisis committees take action.

United Nations Office of Drugs and Crime

The United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) works to address illicit drug trafficking, crime prevention, and international terrorism through research, guidance, and technical/financial assistance to governments.

Cold War

Cold War, the open yet restricted rivalry that developed after World War II between the United States and the Soviet Union and their respective allies. The Cold War was waged on political, economic, and propaganda fronts and had only limited recourse to weapons.

International Press

"IP" refers to the International Press, also known as the Press Corps. This group simulates journalists and media representatives, playing a crucial role in documenting and disseminating the proceedings of the conference.

UN Women

UN Women is the United Nations entity dedicated to gender equality and the empowerment of women. A global champion for women and girls, UN Women was established to accelerate progress on meeting their needs worldwide.

Disarmament and International Security Council

DISEC (Disarmament and International Security Committee) is the First Committee of the United Nations General Assembly. It deals with issues related to disarmament, non-proliferation of weapons, and international security. DISEC's focus includes global peace and stability, with a particular emphasis on threats to international security and the regulation of armaments.

Lok Sabha

The Lok Sabha is the lower house of the Indian Parliament, composed of representatives elected directly by the people. It has a maximum strength of 550 members, with 530 representing states and 20 representing union territories. The Parliament Digital Library provides access to various parliamentary documents, including debates, reports, and publications of the Lok Sabha.
